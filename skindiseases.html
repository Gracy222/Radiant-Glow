Skin Diseases and Treatments

  • Acne

    Treatment: Acne vulgaris is the medical name for common acne -- the presence of blackheads, whiteheads, and other types of pimples on the skin. The most common spots for breakouts are the face, chest, shoulders, and back. Although mild acne may improve with over-the-counter treatments,
    severe forms should be treated by a dermatologist.

  • Eczema

    Treatment: Moisturizers, corticosteroids, antihistamines, and avoiding triggers.

  • Psoriasis

    Treatment: Topical treatments, light therapy, and systemic medications.

  • Rosacea

    Treatment: Topical medications, oral antibiotics, and laser therapy.

  • Skin Cancer

    Treatment: Surgery, radiation therapy, and chemotherapy.

  • Atopic Dermatitis

    Treatment: Emollients, topical corticosteroids, and immunosuppressants.

  • Seborrheic Dermatitis

    Treatment: Antifungal shampoos, topical corticosteroids, and antifungal creams.
